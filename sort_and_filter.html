Sort & Filter

Make a messy list behave in 30 seconds.

5 min read Beginner-friendly Includes practice file

Why this matters

Most of us scroll through long spreadsheets looking for one piece of information, then scroll back up because we lost our place. Sort and Filter fix that. They're two of the most useful features in Excel, and they take about 10 seconds to learn.

The basics

i.

Turn it on

Click any cell in your data, then go to the Data tab and click Filter. You'll see little dropdown arrows appear on every column header.

ii.

Sort

Click the dropdown arrow on any column. Pick Sort A to Z (or smallest to largest for numbers) or Sort Z to A.

iii.

Filter

Click the dropdown, uncheck Select All, then check only the values you want to see. Everything else hides until you clear the filter.

iv.

Search inside the filter

Inside the filter dropdown there's a search box. Type a few letters and it narrows your options instantly. Great for long lists.

v.

Clear a filter

Click the dropdown on a filtered column and choose Clear Filter From…. The little funnel icon shows you which columns are filtered.

vi.

Sort by multiple columns

Data tab → Sort (the big button, not the filter dropdown). Add levels like sort by Department, then by Salary.

Did you know

A few things that surprise people

  • You can filter by color if you've used cell highlighting. The dropdown has a Filter by Color option.
  • Filter dropdowns on date columns automatically group by year, then month, then day. Expand a year and check just the months you want.
  • Keyboard shortcut to toggle filters on and off: Ctrl + Shift + L.
  • Sorting only sorts the rows you've selected. If you accidentally select one column instead of the whole table, you'll scramble your data. Always click a single cell inside the data first and let Excel detect the full range.
